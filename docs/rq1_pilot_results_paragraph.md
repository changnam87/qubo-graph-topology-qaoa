## RQ1 Pilot Results Paragraph

As an initial RQ1 pilot, we evaluated 27 QUBO instances from MaxCut-style and assignment-style families using p = 1 QAOA cost circuits and four local coupling-map topologies. Interaction-edge count showed a strong monotonic association with pre-transpilation circuit depth across the pilot set (Spearman ρ = 0.939). After transpilation, this association remained strong within each topology, with correlations between interaction-edge count and transpiled depth ranging from ρ = 0.932 for the line topology to ρ = 0.944 for the two-dimensional grid topology. These results suggest that basic QUBO interaction-graph descriptors provide a useful first-order explanation of QAOA circuit complexity.

Topology-dependent routing overhead differed substantially across coupling maps. The fully connected reference topology produced the lowest mean transpiled depth (35.11) and zero SWAP insertion. By comparison, the grid, heavy-hex-like, and line topologies produced mean SWAP counts of 12.44, 14.74, and 29.41, respectively. The line topology also showed the largest mean two-qubit overhead (1.374), compared with 1.151 for the grid and 1.180 for the heavy-hex-like topology. Interaction-edge count was strongly associated with SWAP count in the sparse topologies, with Spearman correlations of ρ = 0.957, 0.951, and 0.931 for the grid, heavy-hex-like, and line topologies, respectively. Because the fully connected topology required no SWAP gates, its SWAP-count correlation is undefined. These pilot findings support the feasibility of using QUBO graph descriptors as explanatory variables for topology-aware QAOA compilation metrics, while motivating larger-scale RQ1 experiments with broader QUBO families and instance sizes.
